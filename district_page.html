The District Collector is the chief administrative officer of the district and is responsible for the overall administration, revenue collection, law and order maintenance, and implementation of government schemes and programs.

Chennai, formerly known as Madras, is the capital city of Tamil Nadu and one of India's major metropolitan cities. The city serves as a major cultural, economic and educational center of South India. Chennai is known for its rich cultural heritage, classical music and dance traditions, and is often called the "Detroit of India" due to its automobile industry.

The city is also a major IT hub and houses numerous multinational corporations. Chennai has a tropical wet and dry climate and is located on the Coromandel Coast of the Bay of Bengal. The city is well-connected by road, rail, air and sea, making it an important transportation hub.

Chennai is home to several prestigious educational institutions, healthcare facilities, and cultural landmarks that attract visitors from around the world. The city's economy is driven by automobile manufacturing, information technology, healthcare, and port-related activities.

The district covers an area of 426 square kilometers and has a population of over 7 million people, making it one of the most densely populated districts in Tamil Nadu. The administration is divided into several zones and wards for efficient governance and service delivery.
